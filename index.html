Introduction of Peddamandyam

Peddamandyam Mandal, once part of Chittoor and now Annamayya district, includes 17 Gram Panchayats and 153 habitations. It lies in a tri-junction area of Chittoor, Sri Sathya Sai, and YSR Kadapa districts. The region is surrounded by commons, forests, and streams such as Sadhukonda, Rekkalakonda, and Chinneru. Agriculture, livestock, and wage work are the main livelihoods.

Vision Statement

"Strengthen Institutions and secure tenure over commons and sustainable and climate-resilient livelihoods by restoring commons through collaborative action towards better governance at the block level."

"To strengthen village institutions and secure tenure for sustainable, climate-resilient livelihoods and water conservation, access to social security schemes through collaborative coalition building with diverse stakeholders."

SC: 7.03%, ST: 9.54%
Main Workers: 17,184 (Cultivators: 6,749, Agri Labourers: 7,806)

Agriculture

70% of land is dry and rainfed. Major crops include Groundnut, Tomato, Paddy, Red Gram, and Bajra. Farmers face distress due to erratic rainfall, high input cost, and poor market linkages. Borewell failures and debt are increasing due to groundwater depletion.

Livestock

Most households rear livestock such as cattle, goats, and sheep for income. Livestock offers more steady returns than seasonal agriculture.

Water

Average rainfall is 650 mm, but poor groundwater recharge and overexploitation have led to droughts. Traditional tanks are damaged and insufficient for irrigation. Peddamandyam is among the most drought-prone mandals in the region.
