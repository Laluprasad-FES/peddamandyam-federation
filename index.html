Welcome to Peddamandyam Federation

Empowering rural communities through sustainable commons, inclusive governance, and secure livelihoods.

Introduction

Peddamandyam Mandal, once part of Chittoor and now Annamayya district, includes 17 Gram Panchayats and 153 habitations. It lies in a tri-junction area of Chittoor, Sri Sathya Sai, and YSR Kadapa districts. The region is surrounded by commons, forests, and streams such as Sadhukonda, Rekkalakonda, and Chinneru. Agriculture, livestock, and wage work are the main livelihoods.

Vision Statement

“Strengthen Institutions and secure tenure over commons and sustainable and climate-resilient livelihoods by restoring commons through collaborative action towards better governance at the block level”.

Demography

Total Population: 34,503. Literacy Rate: 57.59%. SC: 7.03%, ST: 9.54%. Majority are small farmers and agricultural workers. 9121 households with a sex ratio of 997 females per 1000 males.

Agriculture

70% of land is dry and rainfed. Major crops include Groundnut, Tomato, Paddy, Red Gram, and Bajra. Farmers face distress from erratic rainfall, high input costs, and declining productivity.

Livestock

Most households rely on dairy farming and rearing of small ruminants like goats and sheep. Livestock supports year-round income.

Water

Average rainfall is 650 mm. Water scarcity is critical due to over-extraction, poor recharge, and damaged traditional tanks. The area is drought-prone and climate-vulnerable.
