Introduction of Peddamandyam

According to the 2011 Census, Peddamandyam Mandal (block) was part of Chittoor district in Andhra Pradesh. In 2022, it became part of the newly formed Annamayya district. The Peddamandyam Mandal in the Chittoor district is located in the tri-junction of the Sri Sathya Sai district, Chittoor, and YSR Kadapa districts. The district headquarters is located approximately 50 KM away from the block administrative office. The Peddamandyam consist with 17 Gram Panchayats covering 153 habitations...

The major source of livelihood in this area is rainfed agriculture supplemented by rainfed livestock rearing and wage employment. The project villages are deprived of assured irrigation source. The area is highly backward. The nearest degree college or a reasonably good hospital is 50 km away in Madanapalle...

As part of Annamayya district’s 19 severely drought-hit mandals in 2024, Peddamandyam exemplifies Rayalaseema’s broader struggle with climate change and inadequate infrastructure.

Demography

Peddamnadyam Mandal consists of 156 habitations and 17 Gram Panchayaths. The region’s elevation ranges from 269 to 3,787 meters above sea level... The total Population is 34,502 with a literacy rate of 57.59%. SC and ST make up 17% of the population...

Agriculture

Around 70% of the land is dry and rainfed. Major crops are Groundnut, Tomato, Paddy, Other vegetables, Red gram, and Bajara. Paddy and Groundnut are predominant crops during Kharif and Rabi. Farmers face issues like low yield, high input cost, transport costs, and borewell failures...

Livestock

Most households depend on livestock. Dairy farming and rearing of small ruminants offer relatively steady income compared to agriculture...

Water

Average rainfall is 650 mm. Groundwater levels are critically low due to geological challenges and over-extraction. Traditional irrigation tanks are damaged or inadequate. The block is marked as drought-prone...
